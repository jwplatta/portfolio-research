# SP500 Cross-Sectional Residual Mean Reversion

This directory contains one baseline script (`v0.py`) and ten follow-on iterations (`v1.py` to `v10.py`) for a residual mean reversion study built on `qstudy`.

The main goals during iteration were:
- Reduce max drawdown duration

The aggregate metrics are written to [`results.csv`](./results.csv). They were generated by [`run_all.py`](./run_all.py).

## Baseline

### `v0`
- Approved baseline using:
  - `universe = qs.download(SP500, "2015-01-01", "2023-12-31")`
  - `factors = qs.download(["SPY", "XLK"], "2015-01-01", "2023-12-31")`
  - `benchmark = qs.download("SPY", "2015-01-01", "2023-12-31")`
- Residual 5-day mean reversion, baseline filters, top-250 liquidity, proportional sizing, daily rebalance, and an equity-curve regime scaler.
- Result:
  - Sharpe `1.1269`
  - Max drawdown duration `265`
  - Max drawdown `-16.12%`
  - Avg daily turnover `0.2259`
  - Benchmark correlation `0.0699`


## Iterations

### `v1`
- Change: residualize against `["SPY", *SECTOR_ETFS]` instead of `["SPY", "XLK"]`
- Result: duration `266`; no useful improvement and Sharpe fell sharply

### `v2`
- Change: volatility-scale the residual reversal signal
- Result: duration worsened to `312` and Sharpe dropped materially

### `v3`
- Change: filter out large residual shocks before sizing
- Result: turnover improved, but duration worsened to `321`

### `v4`
- Change: add rolling beta-neutralization after the shock filter
- Result: no duration benefit over `v3`

### `v5`
- Change: tighten the tradeable universe from top-250 to top-200 liquidity
- Result: duration worsened badly to `497`

### `v6`
- Change: rebalance every 2 days instead of daily
- Result: unstable path with extreme drawdown duration; reject

### `v7`
- Change: add a benchmark trend regime scaler on top of baseline
- Result: first meaningful improvement, duration `262`, lower max drawdown, but Sharpe slipped to `0.9973`

### `v8`
- Change: add residual-volatility regime scaling on top of baseline
- Result: profile stayed close to baseline, but duration was slightly worse at `268`

### `v9`
- Change: require short-term signal confirmation across 3-day and 5-day residual reversals
- Result: strongest duration reduction to `222`, but Sharpe fell too much to `0.8836`

### `v10`
- Change: selected final variant uses a slower, milder benchmark regime overlay (`150/250`, defensive scale `0.75`) plus the baseline equity-curve scaler
- Result: duration `262`, max drawdown `-14.67%`, Sharpe `1.1070`, turnover unchanged, benchmark correlation `0.0710`

## Summary
- The baseline was already well-tuned. Most attempts to shorten drawdown duration damaged Sharpe more than they helped.
- The cleanest improvement came from a slow benchmark regime overlay rather than changing the signal or tradeable universe.
- The best balanced endpoint is `v10`: it reduces longest drawdown duration from `265` to `262` and improves max drawdown from `-16.12%` to `-14.67%`, while keeping turnover flat and benchmark correlation near baseline.

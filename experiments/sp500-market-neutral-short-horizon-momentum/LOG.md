# SP500 Market-Neutral Short-Horizon Momentum

This directory contains a baseline (`v0.py`) and a parameter sweep (`v1.py`) for a short-horizon sector-relative momentum strategy on the SP500 universe.

The signal measures each stock's outperformance relative to its GICS sector over a short return window. Positions are long the top 20 and short the bottom 20 within the liquid universe, rebalanced weekly, and beta-neutralized against SPY.

Aggregate metrics are written to [`results.csv`](./results.csv), generated by running [`v1.py`](./v1.py).

---

## Baseline

### `v0`
- Signal: `stock_5d_return - sector_5d_return`, no skip, equal weight
- Long 20 / short 20, weekly rebalance, sector+beta neutralization
- Result:
  - Sharpe `0.45`
  - Ann. return `4.8%`
  - Max drawdown `-25.7%`
  - Benchmark correlation `0.14`

## Key Findings

### Horizon is the dominant factor
- **20-day window wins decisively** across nearly every other parameter setting. 5d and 10d horizons are materially worse (Sharpe often negative with skip/vol_norm combinations).
- This suggests the signal is capturing medium-term sector-relative drift rather than short-term mean reversion.

### Skip period
- **No skip (s=0) is best at 20d** — Sharpe 1.25 vs 0.73 with skip=5d. The most recent returns add signal, not noise, at this horizon.
- Skip=3d is destructive at 10d and 20d (Sharpe drops sharply, max drawdown worsens to -39% to -65%).
- Skip=5d only helps when combined with vol normalization at 20d.

### Residualization makes no difference
- `res0` and `res1` produce identical results across all combinations. The sector-relative construction already implicitly removes market beta, so stripping SPY returns pre-signal adds nothing.

### Vol normalization
- **Hurts at 20d/no-skip** (Sharpe drops from 1.25 to 0.36). The raw sector-relative signal has a stable enough scale at this horizon.
- **Helps at 20d/skip=5d** — the only combination where vol normalization improves Sharpe (0.86 vs 0.28).

### Weighting
- **Equal weight is best at 20d/no-skip** (Sharpe 1.25 vs 1.05 for inv_vol, 0.71 for proportional).
- **inv_vol reduces drawdown** at 20d/no-skip: max DD improves from -15.3% to -11.5% at the cost of ~3pp annualized return.
- Proportional (Sharpe-weighted) weighting generally underperforms — likely too sensitive to short-window Sharpe estimates.

---

## Summary

The strongest configuration is **20-day window, no skip, equal weight** (Sharpe 1.25, ann. return 15.8%, max DD -15.3%). The v0 baseline at 5d was a poor starting point for momentum — short-horizon sector-relative returns appear to be mean-reverting, not momentum, which explains v0's weak Sharpe of 0.45.

If lower drawdown is preferred over return, **20d/no-skip/inv_vol** (Sharpe 1.05, max DD -11.5%) is the cleanest alternative.

Next steps worth exploring:
- Beta-neutralize at the sector level (not just market) to reduce the benchmark correlation (~0.19) further
- Test on a longer date range to confirm 20d signal stability post-2023
- Regime overlay (VIX or benchmark trend) to reduce the -15% drawdown

## Intraday Exhaustion Reversal

This strategy attempts to identify situations where intraday price moves become temporarily overextended due to panic, forced liquidation, stop cascades, or short-term liquidity imbalances. Typical setups involve unusually large intraday declines or rallies accompanied by extreme realized volatility, high short-term volume, or acceleration late in the trading session. The hypothesis is that these moves overshoot fair value in the short run and partially reverse once liquidity conditions normalize. Common implementations include buying extreme intraday selloffs near the close and exiting the following day, or fading statistically extreme moves relative to recent intraday volatility. This type of strategy is often relatively uncorrelated with medium-term momentum systems because it operates on much shorter horizons and exploits microstructure effects rather than trend persistence.

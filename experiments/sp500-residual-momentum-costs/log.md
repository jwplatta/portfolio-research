- What we found:
  - the biggest improvement came from slowing rebalance from `10` to `15` trading days, then filtering to the strongest `75%+` absolute residual-momentum signals
  - longer residual momentum horizons such as `60/20`, `60/10`, and `90/20` consistently hurt Sharpe despite lower turnover
  - ranking and weighting changes did not improve the current baseline; `equal_vol` was the least-bad alternative, but still below `v0`
  - sector-relative residual momentum, adding sectors to the factor model, and moving to a more concentrated `10/10` book all reduced Sharpe
  - the current bottleneck appears to be gross signal edge and cross-sectional selection quality, not turnover
